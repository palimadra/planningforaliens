The diet is designed to meet two criteria: Improve leptin sensitivity and decrease reward feedback by eating simple, less palatable food" author: "sean" ---

Breakfast, lunch and dinner.

Breakfast is eggs, scrambled with no seasoning, uncured bacon and a side of berries covered in coconut milk. I also drink a few cups of decaf coffee with coconut milk mixed in.

Lunch is a substantial salad with a vinaigrette, a handful of raw almonds, a baked chicken thigh (bone-in with skin) and a banana. The salad is filled with mushrooms, bell peppers, a full avocado and leafy greens (I eat a variety of greens). I eat the salad first and then the other items if I'm hungry.

For dinner I eat a piece of salmon cooked in coconut oil. I chop up summer squash, onions and mushrooms and saute them in coconut oil. No seasoning. I also eat a few forkfuls of sauerkraut.

After dinner I've been drinking a glass of red wine. I take supplements in the morning and before I go to bed: fish oil, d3, magnesium, probiotics, coq10. I drink as much water as I want.

The rules

I do not count calories. I eat as much as I want at each meal. I do not snack between meals. No snacking helps me differentiate between real hunger and simple cravings. I sometimes mistake the two.

It's pretty boring.

This diet meets both of my criteria. It is anti-inflammatory and has low palatability. That's not to say the food isn't tasty. I enjoy the flavors, but not too much. Also, palatability is more than just flavor. Smells and colors and flavor combinations are a part of what determines palatability. Variability is also important. If you eat the same thing over and over, it becomes less palatable. So I eat the same thing every day.

This diet is also low on carbohydrates. This should lower my triglycerides which in turn should increase my leptin sensitivity. Also, low carbohydrate diets make it easier to skip meals or abstain from snacking.

Exercise

Exercise will continue normally. I lift 3 times per week. I do yoga twice a week. I have an intervals class on my bike on Saturdays. I may throw in some running since I've been thinking of doing a triathlon this year. This is the usual amount of exercise I have been doing weekly for the past year.

How does it feel so far?

It's been one week and I've been feeling sluggish. I crash in the afternoons and feel a little blue. I was very hungry between meals without snacks, and I still am, but it's getting easier to wait it out. The hunger is diminishing.

My weight remains unchanged. I took measurements and photos before I started so I will retake those at the end of the experiment. Rest assured: there is zero chance of a shirtless picture of me appearing on the internet. I will just report the results sans picture.
