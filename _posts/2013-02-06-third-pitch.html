I am teaching myself the art of the long-form sales letter or what I'm calling the long-form pitch. In two attempts I have completely failed to connect with my audience. This is what happened after my third attempt.

sudo apt-get upgrade career

Can a robot find you better jobs than a recruiter?

I think we all know the answer is yes. Is it because machine learning algorithms are so advanced? Nope. It's because recruiters suck so bad that even a rudimentary bayesian classifier could probably do a better job.

Think of it as a job-seeking daemon process that emails you when it finds something good.

Tell RecruiterBot what's bothering you. While you're at it, tell him what you want your next job to look like and a few of your favorite programming languages and platforms.

  • Are you happy with your job but would jump on an opportunity to work with an education startup? RecruiterBot will find that startup.
  • Are you ready to move up the ladder a step and lead your own team?
  • Would you love to work for a space rocket company and fling a few things into orbit? RecruiterBot gets it -- space is awesome.

I hate recruiter spam.

I've been programming professionally for over seven years. I love programming. My heart beats a little faster every time I open Emacs to start hacking on a new project. I love making stuff that real people use to make their lives better. It's also nice that I never really have to worry about finding a job: my skills are in demand. Being a software developer certainly has its perks.

But if my skills are in such demand, why do recruiters send me such shitty job opportunities? Most of the time the stuff I get is about one level above pure spam. I think there are a few reasons, but one of the main reasons is they are not programmers and they don't know what we want.

I can't put an end to all recruiter spam, but I can promise this:

  • 1. Any email you get from RecruiterBot will contain an awesome job that should at least perk your interest attention
  • 2. Every email will be written with the care and attention to detail that could only come from RecruiterBot.

I for one welcome our recruiting robot overlords.
