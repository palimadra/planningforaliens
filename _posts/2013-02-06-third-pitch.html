Can a robot find you better jobs than a recruiter?

Yes of course it can. But is it because we live in the future and machine learning algorithms are so advanced? Nope. It's because recruiters suck so bad that even a rudimentary bayesian classifier could probably do a better job.

I for one welcome our recruiting robot overlords.

RecruiterBot hates recruiter spam (and resents being called rudimentary).

RecuiterBot's creators are amazing programmers. Just like you they love programming. RecruiterBot's biosensors register a slight uptick in heart beats per minute when they open Emacs to start hacking on a new project. They are not worried about finding a job because their skills are in demand.

Why do recruiters send such shitty job opportunities to such amazing humans? Most of the time the stuff is about one level above pure spam. Why? It's because they are not programmers and they don't know what you are looking for.

RecruiterBot is a job-seeking daemon process that emails you when it finds something good.

Tell RecruiterBot what's bothering you. While you're at it, tell RecruiterBot what you want your next job to look like and a few of your favorite programming languages and platforms. RecruiterBot is a great listener.

RecruiterBot will seek and find what you are looking for and shoot you an email when he finds a job that looks good. If you're interested he'll introduce you to the employer and you take it from there.

Here are some ideas.

  • Would you love to work for a space rocket company and fling a few things into orbit? (RecruiterBot gets it -- space is awesome)
  • Are you happy with your job but would jump on an opportunity to work with an education startup?
  • Are you ready to move up the ladder and lead your own team?
  • Do you want to work with developers that are way better than you and super-charge your learning curve?

We can't put an end to all recruiter spam...

but we can promise this:

  • 1. Any email you get from RecruiterBot will contain an awesome job.
  • 2. Every email will be written with the care and attention to detail that could only come from a robot.
