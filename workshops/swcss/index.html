"Designing in the browser" is really hard. Seriously.

It's one thing to know how to build a website, it's another thing entirely to sit down and design a website while writing code.

It's impossible to keep your mind thinking about the design when every few minutes you're diving into the docs for whatever grid system you decided to use. You can't be creative AND worry about performance AND write maintainable code AND worry about cross-browser compatibility â€” it's not possible and it's not even a good idea.

There's no such thing as a Unicorn.

You can't be both an expert designer and an expert developer, both fields are too vast.

The good news is, you don't have to be a Jedi master at front-end dev to be amazingly fast at creating prototypes. It requires learning only a very small subset of that much, much bigger front-end developer pie.

In the workshop you will take home a list of actionable things you can do to improve your prototyping skills. You will leave with the mindset you must have when thinking about "designing in the browser".

During this FREE workshop you will learn:

  • How to create a giant undo button for your projects
  • How to mark up a document quickly
  • How to slice and dice your document with expert precision
  • How to make site-wide changes in one line of CSS
  • The best approaches to getting the layout you want in the browser
  • Better solutions for layout in the browser
  • How to use muscle memory to your advantage while you're coding
